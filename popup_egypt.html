ANCIENT EGYPT

ca. 3150 BCE to 332 BCE

Ancient Egypt relied heavily on predictable annual flooding from the Nile river. They developed advanced irrigation techniques to suit their agricultural needs. Their government consisted of a stable central government headed by an absolute monarch, known as the Pharaoh. They had a strict, distinct hierarchy, with the majority of people being low class farmers. Their written language, hieroglyphics, consisted of images and was inscribed on stone, clay, or a paper-like material called papyrus. In addition, Ancient Egyptians had advanced quarrying, surveying, and construction techniques, employed a money barter system using grain, and developed mathematics and effective medical systems.
